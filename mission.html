OUR MISSION

Pioneering the Future of Rendezvous and Proximity Operation Developement

At PRISM, our mission is to design, build, and validate a budget friendly, scalable 3DOF satellite platform that can demonstrate key technologies for safe and precise Rendezvous and Proximity Operations (RPO). As space becomes increasingly crowded, the ability to approach, dock, service, or refuel spacecraft is more critical than ever. PRISM aims to advance these capabilities at the undergraduate level, bridging classroom theory with real-world engineering challenges.

Why RPO Matters

Enabling Sustainability in Space: RPO technologies support satellite servicing, debris mitigation, and life extension of space assets.
Advancing Human Exploration: From docking with the ISS to assembling spacecraft for lunar and Martian missions, RPO is a cornerstone of future exploration.
Strengthening National Security & Industry Leadership: Precision maneuvering and satellite autonomy are key to maintaining leadership and accountability in aerospace and defense.

Our Objectives

Design & Build a 3DOF spacecraft platform capable of simulating in-orbit-plane RPO maneuvers on an air-bearing test bed.
Integrate CO₂ Thrusters & Reaction Wheel Control Authority to achieve controlled mobility and attitude adjustment.
Develop and Validate Algorithms for guidance, navigation, and control (GNC) in basic proximity scenarios.
Demonstrate Proof-of-Concept that can serve as a foundation for future orbital testing and research.
Educate & Inspire the next generation of aerospace engineers by providing a hands-on, research-driven experience.

Our Vision

PRISM seeks to become a model for undergraduate-led space engineering innovation. By combining interdisciplinary collaboration, cutting-edge simulation, and hands-on hardware development, we aim to demonstrate that the future of autonomous spacecraft operations can begin in the lab today.
